# Acknowledgments

The quantitative methods curriculum I encountered as a student gave me
the initial scaffolding for this book. The problem-based learning ethos —
confront the problem before writing the solution — guided the
documentation-first approach.

The 487 works cited across the 48 chapters represent centuries of
accumulated insight from mathematicians, statisticians, economists,
physicists and computer scientists. I have tried to credit sources
faithfully and to point readers towards the original treatments.

The open-source community built the tools that made this work possible.
TypeScript provided the language. Pandoc and LuaLaTeX transformed the
manuscript into print. Mermaid produced the 289 diagrams. SciPy, SymPy and
NumPy served as the reference implementations against which every numerical
result was verified.

Errors that remain are mine. Reports may be filed on the
[errata page](errata.md).
